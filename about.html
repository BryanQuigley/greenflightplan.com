Lead-tax scenarios

These are illustrative scenarios, not predictions. They show how a rising $1/gal fee on 100LL could play out depending on how quickly unleaded alternatives reach the market.

No Tax — Business as Usual

Voluntary transition, no price signal. Without a fee, demand barely budges.

Methodology

We used flights data from BTS T-100 Domestic Segment (U.S. Carriers) data from August 2022. We considered only non-empty Passenger flights (Service Class F) in the contiguous United States, carried out by the biggest US air carriers (as defined by BTS as "Certified U.S. air carriers that account for at least one percent of domestic scheduled passenger revenues"). Airport data is from OurAirports. We only considered medium-size and large airports with scheduled service.

Spoke distances were calculated using airport distance and do not take into account actual flight paths. We considered spokes "replaceable with one additional layover" at a range R if there is at least one intermediate airport that can reach the spoke's origin and destination within the range R.

Emissions were approximated at 144.2g CO₂ per-seat-per-mile based on the IBA Aviation Carbon Emissions Index. We calculated emissions based on passenger count and spoke distance. This does not take into account actual flight paths, empty seats, aircraft or engine types, or secondary effects like contrails.
